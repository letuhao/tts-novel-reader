# English Curriculum Design

## üìö Overview

This document outlines the comprehensive English curriculum for the English Tutor App, structured according to CEFR (Common European Framework of Reference) levels from A1 (Beginner) to C2 (Proficient).

## üéØ Curriculum Philosophy

- **Progressive Learning:** Build from simple to complex
- **Communicative Approach:** Focus on practical communication
- **Adaptive:** Adjust to individual student needs
- **Comprehensive:** Cover all language skills (listening, speaking, reading, writing)
- **Engaging:** Use interactive, real-world content
- **Measurable:** Clear assessment criteria at each level

## üìñ Curriculum Structure

### Level A1: Beginner

**Goal:** Can understand and use familiar everyday expressions and very basic phrases.

#### Vocabulary (500 words)
- Basic personal information (name, age, nationality)
- Numbers (1-100)
- Colors
- Family members
- Common objects (house, food, clothes)
- Days of the week, months
- Basic verbs (be, have, do, go, come, see)

#### Grammar Topics
1. **Present Simple**
   - Affirmative, negative, questions
   - Third person singular (s/es)
   - Common verbs

2. **To Be / To Have**
   - I am, you are, he/she/it is
   - I have, you have, he/she/it has

3. **Articles**
   - A/an/the
   - Zero article

4. **Plurals**
   - Regular plurals (-s, -es)
   - Common irregular plurals

5. **Possessives**
   - My, your, his, her, our, their
   - 's for possession

6. **Prepositions of Place**
   - In, on, at, under, next to

7. **There is/There are**
   - Countable/uncountable introduction

#### Topics/Themes
- Greetings and introductions
- Personal information
- Family and friends
- Daily routines
- Food and drinks
- Shopping (basics)
- Places in town
- Weather
- Hobbies and interests

#### Skills Development

**Listening:**
- Simple instructions
- Basic personal information
- Numbers and prices
- Simple present tense conversations

**Speaking:**
- Introducing yourself
- Asking for basic information
- Describing simple things
- Pronunciation of basic sounds

**Reading:**
- Short simple texts (50-100 words)
- Signs and notices
- Basic forms

**Writing:**
- Simple sentences
- Personal information forms
- Short descriptions (3-5 sentences)

#### Sample Lessons
1. Hello! Nice to meet you
2. Where are you from?
3. My family
4. What do you do?
5. Daily routine
6. What's your favorite food?
7. Shopping for basics
8. Describing your town
9. Weather and seasons
10. My hobbies

### Level A2: Elementary

**Goal:** Can understand sentences and frequently used expressions related to areas of immediate relevance.

#### Vocabulary (1000 words total, +500 new)
- Work and jobs
- Travel and transport
- Health and body
- Education and school
- Entertainment and media
- Emotions and feelings
- Time expressions (past, future)
- Phrasal verbs (basic)

#### Grammar Topics
1. **Past Simple**
   - Regular and irregular verbs
   - Questions and negatives
   - Time expressions (yesterday, last week)

2. **Present Continuous**
   - Actions happening now
   - Future arrangements

3. **Future Forms**
   - Will for predictions
   - Going to for plans
   - Present continuous for arrangements

4. **Comparatives and Superlatives**
   - Big, bigger, biggest
   - More/most
   - As...as

5. **Modal Verbs**
   - Can/can't (ability, permission)
   - Must/mustn't (obligation)
   - Should/shouldn't (advice)

6. **Present Perfect (Introduction)**
   - I have been to...
   - I have done...

7. **Countable/Uncountable Nouns**
   - Some/any
   - Much/many
   - A lot of/lots of

8. **Prepositions of Time**
   - At, in, on
   - Before, after, during

#### Topics/Themes
- Past experiences
- Plans and intentions
- Describing people and places
- Jobs and work
- Travel experiences
- Health and fitness
- Shopping and money
- Technology
- Entertainment (movies, music, books)
- Education and learning

#### Skills Development

**Listening:**
- Simple past conversations
- Future plans
- Descriptions of people/places
- Basic phone conversations

**Speaking:**
- Talking about past experiences
- Making plans
- Describing people and places
- Giving opinions (simple)

**Reading:**
- Short texts (100-200 words)
- Simple stories
- Basic news articles
- Instructions

**Writing:**
- Personal stories
- Simple emails
- Descriptions (paragraphs)
- Short messages and notes

#### Sample Lessons
1. What did you do yesterday?
2. Making plans for the weekend
3. Comparing people and places
4. My job
5. A trip I took
6. Staying healthy
7. Shopping and spending
8. Technology in daily life
9. Movies and entertainment
10. School days

### Level B1: Intermediate

**Goal:** Can understand the main points of clear standard input on familiar matters regularly encountered.

#### Vocabulary (2000 words total, +1000 new)
- Abstract concepts
- Academic vocabulary (beginning)
- Idioms (common)
- Phrasal verbs (extended)
- Collocations
- Word formation (prefixes, suffixes)

#### Grammar Topics
1. **Present Perfect vs Past Simple**
   - When to use each
   - Time expressions

2. **Present Perfect Continuous**
   - Actions continuing to present
   - I have been studying...

3. **Past Continuous**
   - Actions in progress in the past
   - Interrupted actions

4. **Past Perfect**
   - Actions before other past actions

5. **Future Forms (Extended)**
   - Future continuous
   - Future perfect
   - Conditionals (zero, first)

6. **Modals (Extended)**
   - Could, might, may (possibility)
   - Should have, could have
   - Must have (deduction)

7. **Passive Voice**
   - Present and past passive
   - By + agent

8. **Relative Clauses**
   - Who, which, that, where
   - Defining and non-defining

9. **Conditionals**
   - Zero conditional (general truths)
   - First conditional (real future)
   - Second conditional (hypothetical)

10. **Reported Speech**
    - Say, tell, ask
    - Tense changes

#### Topics/Themes
- Work and career
- Education and training
- Society and culture
- Health and lifestyle
- Technology and innovation
- Environment and nature
- Media and communication
- Relationships
- Travel and tourism
- Food and cooking

#### Skills Development

**Listening:**
- Extended conversations
- Lectures and presentations (simple)
- Radio/TV programs (main points)
- Different accents

**Speaking:**
- Expressing opinions
- Agreeing/disagreeing
- Describing experiences in detail
- Participating in discussions

**Reading:**
- Articles (300-500 words)
- Stories and novels (adapted)
- Reports and reviews
- Understanding main ideas and details

**Writing:**
- Opinion essays (3-4 paragraphs)
- Formal and informal emails
- Stories and narratives
- Reports and summaries

#### Sample Lessons
1. My career goals
2. Education systems
3. Cultural differences
4. Healthy lifestyle choices
5. Technology's impact
6. Environmental issues
7. Social media and communication
8. Building relationships
9. Travel experiences
10. Cooking and food culture

7. **Participle Clauses**
   - -ing and -ed participles
   - Reducing clauses

8. **Inversion**
   - Negative inversion
   - Conditional inversion

9. **Emphasis**
   - Cleft sentences

## üéì Curriculum Components

### 1. Core Lessons

Each level contains:
- **20-30 core lessons** covering main grammar and vocabulary
- **10-15 topic-based lessons** for practical application
- **5-10 review lessons** for consolidation

### 2. Exercise Types

- **Grammar exercises**
  - Fill in the blanks
  - Multiple choice
  - Error correction
  - Sentence transformation

- **Vocabulary exercises**
  - Matching
  - Definitions
  - Context usage
  - Word formation

- **Speaking exercises**
  - Pronunciation practice
  - Conversation practice
  - Role plays
  - Presentations

- **Listening exercises**
  - Comprehension questions
  - Dictation
  - Gap filling
  - Note-taking

- **Reading exercises**
  - Comprehension questions
  - True/false
  - Vocabulary in context
  - Summary writing

- **Writing exercises**
  - Sentence writing
  - Paragraph writing
  - Essay writing
  - Creative writing

### 3. Assessment System

#### Placement Test
- Assess current level
- Identify strengths/weaknesses
- Recommend starting point

#### Progress Tests
- End of each unit
- Skills-based assessment
- Level progression check

#### Final Exam
- Comprehensive level test
- All four skills
- CEFR-aligned certification

### 4. Adaptive Learning Features

- **Personalized Path:** Adjust based on performance
- **Weak Area Focus:** Extra practice for struggling topics
- **Acceleration:** Skip mastered content
- **Remediation:** Review when needed
